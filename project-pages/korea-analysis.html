South Korea COVID-19 Exploratory Data Analysis
This project analyzes COVID-19 datasets for South Korea using libraries like Pandas, NumPy, Scipy, and Plotly. The exploration looks into case distribution, patient information, time trends, demographics, and government policies. Interactive visualizations are created using Plotly on various dataframes, each focusing on specific aspects of the pandemic in South Korea.
Summary / Findings
This COVID-19 Data Analysis Project for South Korea uncovers insights from different dataframes. Daegu is the most affected province, with Nam-Gu in Daegu being the most impacted city. Group infections contribute significantly to high case numbers, linked to overseas travel and contact with infected individuals. The PatientInfo DataFrame highlights a predominant female patient profile, cases related to overseas inflow, and a pattern of infections through contact with individuals, often within close-knit communities.
Analysis of the Time DataFrame shows a peak in confirmed cases in February 2020, with a steady increase in released cases and a relatively stable number of deceased cases. The TimeAge DataFrame displays a consistent rise in confirmed cases across all age groups, with the 20s age group having the highest number of cases. Data by gender in the TimeGender DataFrame indicates a higher incidence of confirmed cases among males, with a corresponding higher death rate. The TimeProvince DataFrame identifies Daegu as the epicenter of COVID-19. The Region DataFrame uncovers variations in elderly population ratios across provinces.
Additionally, the Weather DataFrame shows a weak positive correlation between average temperature and confirmed cases. SearchTrend data reflects heightened interest in "Coronavirus" in March 2020, and the SeoulFloating DataFrame, when log-transformed, reveals fluctuations in Seoul's floating population. Lastly, the Policy DataFrame highlights government initiatives aiming to reduce virus spread, safeguard public health, and support affected individuals and businesses.
