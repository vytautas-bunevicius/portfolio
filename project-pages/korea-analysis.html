South Korea COVID-19 Exploratory Data Analysis
This project utilizes Pandas, Numpy, Scipy, and Plotly libraries to analyze and clean COVID-19-related datasets specific to South Korea. The exploration covers diverse aspects such as case distribution, patient information, time trends, demographics, and government policies. Additionally, the analysis involves the creation of interactive visualizations using Plotly on multiple dataframes, each focusing on specific aspects of the pandemic in South Korea.
Summary / Findings
In this comprehensive COVID-19 Data Analysis Project focused on South Korea, various dataframes provided valuable insights into the multifaceted impact of the pandemic. Among the key findings, Daegu emerged as the most severely affected province, with Nam-Gu in Daegu standing out as the most impacted city. Group infections played a significant role in the high case numbers, predominantly attributed to overseas travel and contact with infected individuals.
The PatientInfo DataFrame highlighted a dominant female patient profile, a substantial number of cases related to overseas inflow, and a prevalent pattern of infections through contact with individuals, often within close-knit communities. Examining the Time DataFrame revealed a peak in confirmed cases in February 2020, accompanied by a steady increase in released cases and a relatively stable number of deceased cases. The TimeAge DataFrame showcased a consistent rise in confirmed cases across all age groups, with the 20s age group experiencing the highest number of cases.
Dissecting the data by gender in the TimeGender DataFrame indicated a higher incidence of confirmed cases among males, with a corresponding higher death rate. The TimeProvince DataFrame identified Daegu as the epicenter of COVID-19, with the highest case count, while the Region DataFrame uncovered variations in elderly population ratios across provinces. Additionally, the Weather DataFrame demonstrated a weak positive correlation between average temperature and confirmed cases. SearchTrend data reflected heightened interest in "Coronavirus" in March 2020, and the SeoulFloating DataFrame, when log-transformed, revealed fluctuations in Seoul's floating population.
Lastly, the Policy DataFrame underscored government initiatives aimed at reducing virus spread, safeguarding public health, and supporting affected individuals and businesses.
