Coursera EDA: An Exploratory Data Analysis of Coursera Courses
In this project, I looked at Coursera’s courses using data from their API. I cleaned the data, used statistics, and made visuals to find patterns in course ratings, how many students sign up, and what kinds of certificates they get.
Summary / Findings
I found out that most Coursera courses offer a “COURSE” certificate, with “SPECIALIZATION” and “PROFESSIONAL CERTIFICATE” also available. The courses are mostly for beginners or those at an intermediate level, not so much for advanced learners.
The study shows which organizations have a lot of students, with “SPECIALIZATION” certificates being tougher and “PROFESSIONAL CERTIFICATE” certificates for beginners only. It seems that the easier courses get better ratings, while the harder ones don’t, maybe because they’re not as common or people expect more from them.
I also noticed that some courses have the same name but are actually different, which means we need to look closer at those. “COURSE” certificates are the most common, and it looks like people like getting certificates that show they’re good at certain skills or subjects.
Most courses are easy or medium-hard, which means Coursera has something for everyone. The study also found a small link between how students rate courses and how many sign up. But just because two things are linked doesn’t mean one causes the other; there could be other reasons why more students sign up.
To sum up, this analysis gives us a full picture of what Coursera offers, including what kinds of certificates people like, how hard the courses are, and how ratings and student numbers are connected.
