Coursera EDA: An Exploratory Data Analysis of Coursera Courses
This exploratory data analysis (EDA) delves into the distribution of courses on Coursera, utilizing data from the Coursera API. The analysis encompasses various data preprocessing steps, descriptive statistics, and visualizations to uncover patterns related to course ratings, student enrollments, and certificate types.
Summary / Findings
The goal of this exploratory data analysis (EDA) is to understand the Coursera dataset and identify key trends. Using Python libraries like NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn, the analysis involves cleaning the dataset, checking for missing values and duplicates, and transforming features for better efficiency. The examination covers descriptive statistics, distributions, outliers, and correlations, providing insights into course ratings, student enrollments, and more.
The analysis discovers patterns such as the dominance of certain certificate types, the popularity of organizations, and the distribution of difficulty levels. Visualizations highlight certificate type distribution, difficulty level distribution, and the relationship between difficulty levels and certificate types. The results indicate that Coursera offers a diverse range of courses for learners of all levels, with SPECIALIZATION certificates being more challenging and PROFESSIONAL CERTIFICATE certificates less dominant.
In summary, this EDA reveals the varied landscape of Coursera courses, offering insights into certificate preferences, difficulty distributions, and organizational prominence. It underscores the platform's commitment to providing courses for learners with different interests and skill levels.
